RAFFLES HISTORY

Raffles Hall was the one of the first halls to be established in the National University of Singapore (NUS), and consequently has a long and rich history.

Founded in 1958, Raffles Hall was originally located in Nassim Road, Bukit Timah, alongside Eusoff College. At the time, Raffles Hall was an all boys hall, while Eusoff was for the ladies. However, due to an increase in the number of female students, Raffles Hall officially became co-ed in 1975.

Then, in the 1981, NUS shifted from the Bukit Timah campus to the current Kent Ridge campus. Kent Ridge Hall and Sheares Hall were already on campus at the time, and the convenience of these new halls took its toll on the residential numbers in RH. Consequently, Raffles Hall bade Nassim Road farewell and moved to the Kent Ridge campus in 1984.

Whereas Nassim could only hold 250-odd residents, Raffles Hall at its new site is almost twice that size. New kids on campus with nothing much to show, spurred on by the old and the new, dreamers and makers began to bud, setting in motion the cogs of our tradition and history…

Raffles Hall has its roots in Raffles College, which was one of the institutes of higher learning that merged to eventually form the National University of Singapore (the other being King Edward VII college). Raffles Hall was also modelled after the colleges at Oxford and Cambridge, and hence many of their traditions trickled down to us. The positions of the Master, Resident Fellows, Junior Common Room Committees (JCRCs) and Senior Common Room Committees (SCRCs) all stemmed from those origins. And although “Raffles” is a common name for many institutions and organisations in Singapore, Raffles Hall is in no way affiliated to them.
